Quick overview

The SSC Tuatara is an American hypercar engineered for extreme top speed, lightweight design and aerodynamic efficiency. Its twin-turbo V8 and ultra-slippery bodywork put it among the fastest cars ever built.

History — how it came to be

The Tuatara project began after the success of the SSC Ultimate Aero, which once held the title of the world’s fastest production car. SSC aimed to surpass its own record by creating a next-generation hypercar with cutting-edge aerodynamics, a powerful bespoke engine, and a futuristic design.

Development took several years, with SSC partnering with top engineers for carbon construction, wind-tunnel refinement, and powertrain optimization. The result was the Tuatara — a hypercar designed to challenge 300+ mph territory while retaining road legality and advanced driver-focused engineering.

Key specifications

  • Engine: 5.9L twin-turbo V8
  • Power: 1,350 hp (regular fuel) / 1,750+ hp (E85)
  • Top speed claim: 300+ mph (varies by testing)
  • Acceleration: Extremely rapid, low-drag configuration
  • Weight: ~1,250 kg (carbon-fiber body)
  • Transmission: 7-speed roboticized system
  • Drive: Rear-wheel drive
  • Production: Very limited

Why it matters

The SSC Tuatara represents America’s push into the top-speed hypercar competition. With radical aerodynamics, a screaming twin-turbo V8 and ultra-lightweight engineering, it stands as one of the most ambitious speed machines ever produced.
