Quick overview

The Bugatti Veyron Super Sport is the extreme high-performance version of the legendary Veyron. With its quad-turbocharged W16 engine and advanced aerodynamics, it once held the title of the world’s fastest production car.

History — how it came to be

The original Bugatti Veyron, launched in 2005, shocked the world by reaching speeds never before achieved by a road-legal car. But Bugatti wanted to push the limits even further.

In 2010, the Veyron Super Sport was unveiled. Engineers increased its power, refined its aerodynamics, and strengthened its structure to handle much higher speeds. The result was a 431 km/h (268 mph) machine — fast enough to set an official Guinness World Record.

Only **30 units** were produced, making it extremely rare and one of the most iconic hypercars of all time.

Key specifications

  • Engine: 8.0L Quad-Turbo W16
  • Power: 1,200 hp
  • Top speed: 431 km/h (limited to 415 km/h for customers)
  • 0–100 km/h: ~2.5 seconds
  • Torque: 1,500 Nm
  • Drive: All-wheel drive
  • Transmission: 7-speed dual-clutch
  • Production: 30 units

Why it matters

The Veyron Super Sport proved that Bugatti was still the king of hypercar engineering. Its combination of luxury, technology, and extreme speed set standards that influenced the entire industry.
