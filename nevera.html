Quick overview

The Rimac Nevera is a fully electric hypercar that delivers insane acceleration, cutting-edge battery technology, and unmatched control using individual motors at each wheel. It's one of the fastest accelerating cars ever made.

History — how it came to be

Rimac began as a small Croatian startup founded by Mate Rimac, an engineer passionate about electric performance. Over time, the company developed advanced battery systems and electric drivetrains that caught global attention.

The Nevera was created to showcase Rimac’s engineering power. Designed with a carbon monocoque, a high-density battery pack, and four independent motors, it brings Formula-1-like responsiveness to a road-legal hypercar.

Key specifications

  • Motors: 4 electric motors (one per wheel)
  • Total power: 1,914 hp
  • Top speed: ~412 km/h
  • 0–100 km/h: ~1.9 seconds
  • Battery: 120 kWh lithium-manganese pack
  • Range: ~500+ km (varies by driving)
  • Drive: All-wheel torque-vectoring
  • Production: Limited to 150 units

Why it matters

The Nevera proves that electric hypercars can outperform combustion engines in acceleration, control, and efficiency. It blends luxury with technology and sets multiple world records, making it one of the most advanced cars ever built.
