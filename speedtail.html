Quick overview

The McLaren Speedtail is the fastest McLaren ever made, combining a hybrid powertrain with advanced aerodynamics. Designed as a hyper-GT, it balances extreme top speed with long-distance comfort and futuristic styling.

History — how it came to be

Unveiled in 2018, the Speedtail was McLaren’s answer to a new class of hypercars that emphasize top speed over track performance. Inspired by the F1’s legacy, the Speedtail stretches the cabin and smooths the body to minimize drag.

With a limited production of 106 units, it was designed to be both collectible and a showcase of McLaren’s engineering capabilities. Its central driving position and advanced hybrid system allow it to achieve speeds of up to 250 mph (403 km/h).

Key specifications

  • Engine: 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 + Electric Motor
  • Total power: 1,035 hp
  • Top speed: 250 mph (403 km/h)
  • 0–100 km/h: ~2.9 seconds
  • Transmission: 7-speed dual-clutch
  • Drive: Rear-wheel drive
  • Construction: Carbon-fiber monocoque
  • Production: 106 units

Why it matters

The Speedtail redefines McLaren’s hyper-GT philosophy: extreme speed without sacrificing comfort or long-distance usability. It is a limited-production car that blends aerodynamics, hybrid power, and luxury into a unique experience.
