Quick overview

The Chiron Super Sport 300+ is Bugatti's extreme, limited-run derivative of the Chiron that celebrates the brand's 300+ mph achievement. It pairs immense power with aerodynamic changes and a distinctive lacquered carbon look with orange accents.

History — how it came to be

In 2019 Bugatti developed a special, high-speed derivative of the Chiron after a pre-production prototype exceeded 300 mph on the VW Ehra-Lessien test track. That record run and the engineering work behind it led Bugatti to produce the Chiron Super Sport 300+ as a strictly limited model for collectors.

The project required bespoke aerodynamics, modified gearing, upgraded engine output and specially developed tires; Bugatti worked with partners (including Dallara and Michelin) to make the car stable and safe at extreme speeds. The production Super Sport 300+ retained many of those changes while being adapted for customer use.

Key specifications

  • Engine: 8.0L quad-turbo W16
  • Power: ~1,578 bhp (1,600 PS in marketing figures)
  • Top speed (prototype run): 304.77 mph (≈490.48 km/h)
  • Production run: Extremely limited (30 customer cars announced)
  • 0–100 km/h: ~2.3 s (varies by source)
  • Drive: All-wheel drive
  • Body: Lacquered carbon fiber with signature stripes
  • Approx price: Multi-million euros

Why it matters

The Chiron Super Sport 300+ represents one of the pinnacles of internal-combustion hypercar engineering: combining raw power, precision aerodynamics and tire technology to push a road-legal production-based car beyond 300 mph. It is as much a demonstration of engineering capability and craftsmanship as it is a collectible car.
