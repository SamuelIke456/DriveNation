Quick overview

The Jesko Absolut is Koenigsegg’s top-speed–focused hypercar, designed with the lowest drag coefficient in their history. With immense power and advanced aerodynamics, it aims to break the world top-speed record.

History — how it came to be

Koenigsegg introduced the Jesko in 2019 as the successor to the Agera RS. While the standard Jesko focused on track performance and downforce, Koenigsegg wanted another version dedicated purely to raw speed.

The Jesko Absolut was created with redesigned bodywork, covered rear wheels, a stretched tail, and a drastically reduced drag coefficient of only **0.278** — making it the slipperiest Koenigsegg ever.

Koenigsegg believes the Jesko Absolut can reach over **330–350 mph (531–563 km/h)** in the right conditions, potentially becoming the fastest production car ever made.

Key specifications

  • Engine: 5.0L Twin-Turbo V8
  • Power: 1,600 hp (on E85 fuel)
  • Top speed: 330+ mph target (531+ km/h)
  • 0–100 km/h: ~2.3 seconds
  • Transmission: 9-speed Light Speed Transmission
  • Drag Coefficient: 0.278
  • Drive: Rear-wheel drive
  • Construction: Carbon-fiber monocoque

Why it matters

The Jesko Absolut represents the absolute limit of Koenigsegg engineering. It’s not built for track racing — it’s built to rewrite the definition of top speed in a road-legal car. If it achieves its projected numbers, it will become the fastest production vehicle in history.
